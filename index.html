Course Offerings Heatmap & Table

How to Read This View

The heatmap counts how many classes start in each day and half-hour time slot. Calculation: cell count = distinct CRNs whose meeting day includes that row and whose start time rounds down into that half-hour column. A CRN may appear in multiple cells when it truly meets on multiple days or at different times, but repeated meeting rows, non-recurring dates, or paired-room rows for the same CRN/day/start bucket are counted once. Darker cells mean more course starts at that moment. Click a non-empty cell to show only the matching sections in the table below.

The table supports the heatmap by showing the actual courses behind each count. Used together, the heatmap shows the pattern while the table shows the underlying schedule records, so a busy cell can be investigated immediately instead of treated as an abstract number.

Optional metric modes keep the same day/start-time layout: Section count counts distinct CRNs, Enrollment-weighted sums census/current enrollment once per CRN per bucket, Seat capacity sums section capacity once per CRN per bucket, and Fill-rate shows enrollment divided by capacity after that deduplication. Prime time is Monday through Thursday, 9:00 AM through 2:59 PM. Underutilized periods use the current planning threshold of less than 70% fill. Online/TBA rows using a 00:00-00:59 placeholder block are excluded from physical start-time heatmap counts.

Room Utilization Map

Scores weight room usage between 9:00 AM and 3:00 PM Monday-Thursday more heavily, then adjust expectations by room type and capacity. Expected weighted = weekly weighted availability x room-type target x capacity factor. VISFSC rooms are excluded from this view.

Calculation and Designation Guide

Element How It Is Calculated Why It Matters
Score Weighted hours divided by expected weighted hours. A score near 1.00 means the room is being used near its adjusted expectation.
Weekly hours Total scheduled room hours across selected meeting days for the loaded term data. Shows the raw amount of weekly room use before peak-hour or room-type weighting.
Peak hours Scheduled room hours that fall between 9:00 AM and 3:00 PM on Monday-Thursday. Highlights use during the highest-demand part of the day.
Weighted hours All hours count 1.0x; Monday-Thursday peak minutes between 9:00 AM and 3:00 PM receive an extra 0.5x bump, for 1.5x total. Gives extra value to room use during peak demand while still counting other scheduled use.
Expected weighted Weekly weighted availability x room-type target x capacity factor. Weekly weighted availability is 60.0 hours: Monday-Thursday each have 6 peak hours at 1.5x plus 3 off-peak hours at 1.0x, and Friday has 9 off-peak hours at 1.0x. Sets a fair target so specialized rooms and small rooms are not judged like large general classrooms.
Peak share Peak hours divided by total weekly hours. Shows what percentage of the room's use happens during the Monday-Thursday 9:00 AM-3:00 PM demand window.
Designation Score / Condition Meaning
Not Utilized No scheduled weekly hours No loaded sections are assigned to this room for the selected term data.
Very Efficient Score 1.10 or higher Strong use relative to the adjusted target, especially valuable when peak-hour use is present.
Efficient Score 0.75 to 1.09 Healthy use close to the expected range for that room type and capacity.
Moderately Utilized Score 0.45 to 0.74 Some use, but below the preferred range for regular scheduling.
Under Utilized Score below 0.45 Low use compared with the adjusted expectation.
Small Room Caution Capacity below 20 and 12+ weekly scheduled hours The room is being used regularly, but its size may limit its scheduling value.

Modality Balance

This view uses the Instructional Method column from the uploaded CSV and counts unique CRNs when available, so multi-meeting sections are not counted more than once. Class counts and enrollment are shown as separate comparison views because a modality can have a similar number of sections but materially different student enrollment. Dual Enrollment is excluded by default because it can materially change modality balance; use the checkbox to include it intentionally.

Course Duration Graph

How to Read This View

The Course Duration Graph counts how many classes are active during each half-hour interval by day of week. Calculation: interval count = sections whose meeting day includes that line and whose meeting time overlaps that half-hour interval. A section contributes to every half-hour slot it overlaps, so this view shows classroom demand across the span of class meetings rather than only the start time.

This graph and the heatmap answer different questions. The heatmap shows when classes begin, while the duration graph shows how long demand remains active. Reviewing both together makes it easier to spot whether congestion is caused by many simultaneous starts, long overlapping meetings, or both.
